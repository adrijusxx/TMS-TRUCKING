Phase 1: The Financial "Container" (Load Creation)Objective: Establish the Baseline Revenue and Expected Expense.Your system must create two distinct financial buckets at the moment of load creation:Revenue Bucket (Accounts Receivable): What the Broker/Shipper pays you.Logic: $TotalRevenue = LineHaul + FuelSurcharge + Accessorials$Validation Rule: The system must not allow a load to be marked "Active" without a confirmed Rate (even if $0 for internal moves).Expense Bucket (Accounts Payable): What you will pay the Driver.Logic: This starts as an estimate based on the assigned driver's pay profile (e.g., $\$0.60/mile \times 1200 miles$).Validation Rule: Check for "Duplicate Reference Numbers" (PO# or PU#) to prevent double-booking.Phase 2: The Execution Logic (The "accumulator")Objective: Capture variable costs and revenue adjustments during the trip.As the truck moves, the financial numbers change. Your logic must capture these delta events.Getty ImagesA. Accessorial Triggers (Revenue Add-ons)Your system needs a "Pending Approval" queue for extra charges. Drivers claim them; Dispatch approves them; Accounting bills them.Lumper Fees:Input: Driver uploads receipt image + Amount.Logic: Add to Revenue (pass-through to broker) AND add to Driver Reimbursement (if driver paid cash/personal card) OR mark as Company Paid (if Comcheck used).Detention:Logic: IF (Depart - Arrive) > 2 hours THEN Alert Dispatch.Critical Check: Do not auto-add this to the Invoice. It requires a revised Rate Confirmation from the broker. Create a "Billing Hold" status until the new Rate Con is received.B. This is the most complex part of TMS logic.You need a loop that runs weekly (e.g., every Friday for the previous week's work).Step A: Gross Pay CalculationDepending on the driver type, the math changes:Scenario 1: Mileage Pay (CPM)$$Pay = (LoadedMiles + EmptyMiles) \times DriverRate$$Validation: Which mileage logic? (PC Miler Practical, Shortest, or Odometer?). Stick to PC Miler Practical to avoid disputes.Scenario 2: Percentage Pay$$Pay = (GrossLineHaul \times DriverPercentage)$$Logic Trap: Do you pay % on Fuel Surcharge? Usually No.Correct Formula: $$Pay = (TotalInvoice - FuelSurcharge) \times DriverPercentage$$Step B: Additions & ReimbursementsStop Pay: Flat fee for extra stops (e.g., $50 per extra drop).Reimbursements: Lumper fees, Scales, Tolls (verified by receipts).Step C: Deductions (The "Net Pay" Logic)You must subtract these in a specific priority order:Advances: (Fuel/Cash taken during the trip).Recurring Deductions: Truck Lease, Occupational Accident Insurance, ELD subscription fee.Escrow: (e.g., $100/week until $2500 is held for damages).Fuel Usage: If Owner-Operator, deduct fuel purchased on company card.Step D: Final OutputSettlement Sheet: A PDF breakdown for the driver.ACH File: A CSV file formatted for the bank (NACHA format) to trigger direct deposits.6. The "Unhappy Path" Logic (Where bugs happen)Ensure your system handles these two specific nightmares:1. The "Repower" (Split Load)Truck A breaks down. Truck B finishes the load.The Problem: The Customer pays 1 Invoice. You pay 2 Drivers.The Logic:Create Segment 1: Origin $\to$ Breakdown Location (Pay Driver A).Create Segment 2: Breakdown Location $\to$ Destination (Pay Driver B).Revenue stays attached to the Master Load ID.Driver Pay is calculated per Segment.2. Post-Payment AdjustmentsThe driver was paid last Friday, but today you received a speeding ticket or a cargo claim for that load.The Logic: Do not edit the old Settlement. Create a "Negative Adjustment" (Deduction) on the Next Available Settlement.
